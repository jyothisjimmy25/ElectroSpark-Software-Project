Full Wave Bridge Rectifier

Procedure in lab

The circuit diagram of the bridge diagram is shown shown in Figure 1.

                                                                               Figure 1

 

 

Connections are made on a breadboard as shown in the figure.The four Semiconductor diodes D1, D2, D3, D4 are connected as shown as in figure(similar to Wheatston's bridge).The anode(positive end) of D1 and the cathode(negative end) of are connected to the 1st(0V) terminal of the secondary of the transformer. Similarly the anode(positive end) of D2 and cathode(negative end) are connected to the 2nd terminal(6V). The cathodes(negative ends) of D1 and D2 are connected to the positive of the electrolytic capacitor. The anodes(positive ends) of D3 and D4 are connected to the negative of the electrolytic capacitor.The load resistance (terminals across the resistance box) RL is connected parallel to the capacitor. The output is developed across the load resistance. The primary of the transformer is connected to a 220V a.c mains. The d.c voltge Vd.c developed across RL is measured using a multimeter in d.c voltage in d.c voltage mode. The a.c voltage prent across RL is also measured using the multimeter. Then the ripple factor can be calculated. The d.c and a.c voltage measurements across RL are made by changing the value of RL and the ripple factor is determined in each case. Then the filter capacitor is removed and the above measurements are repeated and the ripple factor without filter is calculated.
